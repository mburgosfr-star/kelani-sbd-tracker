# Kelani SBD Tracker post-v1 product direction

## Milestone

Shortly after the first public 1.0 release, Kelani received its first external GitHub feedback from a real user.

Issue #2 requested Metric/Imperial unit support, especially for users who train in pounds.

This is an important product milestone. Kelani is now receiving feedback from outside its initial development and testing loop, and that feedback should help guide the product direction.

## Direction

From this point forward, product decisions should prioritize:

1. User experience from onboarding onward
2. Readiness for users who train in pounds
3. Clear support and feedback flows
4. Trust, privacy, and simplicity
5. New-user clarity over adding unnecessary complexity

## Product principles

### First-run experience

A new user should quickly understand:

- what Kelani does
- what they need to enter
- why unit choice matters
- what today's workout means
- how the program progresses

Onboarding should become clearer, calmer, and more explanatory without becoming long or distracting.

### International and US readiness

Users who train in pounds should feel that lb support is native, not added as an afterthought.

Kelani should support this by:

- allowing kg/lb choice during onboarding
- keeping unit display and editing consistent across the app
- using clear English terminology
- avoiding assumptions based only on Dutch or European powerlifting contexts

### Support and trust

Support should feel serious and reachable.

The Settings support section, website, GitHub issues, release notes, and public communication should all support the same message:

Kelani is a simple, privacy-friendly strength app that listens to users.

### Feature discipline

New features should be judged by this question:

Does this make Kelani better for a real new user?

Not every useful idea belongs in the next release. After v1.2.0, the next major product focus should likely be onboarding and support, not more training complexity.

## v1.2.0 meaning

v1.2.0 should be treated as the first user-feedback-driven release.

Main theme:

- kg/lb support based on external feedback
- Floor Press as a safer temporary Bench Press alternative
- polish and fixes discovered during real testing
