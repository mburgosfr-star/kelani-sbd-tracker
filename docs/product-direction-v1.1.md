# Kelani SBD Tracker product direction after v1.0

This document captures the product direction for Kelani SBD Tracker after v1.0.

Kelani SBD Tracker has reached a stable foundation: structured powerlifting workouts, cycle progression, meet preparation, optional accessories, body data, stats, local backup/export, and offline-first use.

The next step is not to add features randomly. The next step is to make Kelani more useful, clearer, and more guiding while protecting the principles that make it different.

## Core principles

Kelani should remain:

- offline-first
- no ads
- no tracking
- no account required
- practical in the gym
- focused on squat, bench press, and deadlift
- useful for long-term powerlifting progress

These principles are not secondary. They are part of the product.

## Why Kelani exists

Many lifting apps are useful, polished, and successful. The best ones often do a few things very well:

- they make the next workout obvious
- they reduce guesswork
- they show progress clearly
- they make training feel structured
- they help the user stay consistent

Kelani should learn from these strengths without copying another app’s identity, business model, or limitations.

Kelani exists because a lifter may need more than a simple beginner progression:

- better long-term structure
- clearer powerlifting-specific progression
- meet preparation
- local-first privacy
- flexible but calm training tools
- no subscription pressure for the core experience

Kelani’s position:

> A calm, privacy-respecting powerlifting guide for lifters who want structure, progress, and control.

## Product opportunity

Kelani should become less like a passive tracker and more like a calm training guide.

That does not mean adding noise.

It means helping the lifter understand:

- what to do today
- why the workout looks this way
- whether training is moving in the right direction
- what to do when a lift stalls
- how a cycle is progressing
- how training connects to meet preparation

## What should stay simple

The workout screen must stay simple.

During training, the user should not have to think too much. The app should be fast, clear, and predictable.

Important workout principles:

- compact set layout
- clear weights and reps
- obvious complete/fail/edit controls
- useful rest timer
- no unnecessary explanations during hard training
- no visual clutter
- no surprise behavior

The gym flow matters more than decorative UI.

## What can become smarter

The app can be more explanatory before and after training.

Good places for guidance:

- onboarding
- program overview
- cycle overview
- workout completion
- failed set handling
- cycle completion
- stats interpretation
- meet preparation

The principle:

> Simple during the workout. Smarter around the workout.

## v1.1 focus

v1.1 should focus on guidance, clarity, and feedback.

### 1. Better onboarding

The first-run experience should explain:

- what Kelani is
- who it is for
- what the app will ask from the user
- how training progression works
- why maxes and body data matter
- how the user should start

Goal: the user starts with confidence.

### 2. Clearer program explanation

The app should explain the training structure in simple terms:

- why top sets exist
- why back-off sets exist
- why not every workout is maximal
- why some weeks feel lighter
- how progression works over a cycle
- how meet preparation differs from normal training

Goal: the user trusts the program instead of only following numbers.

### 3. Better progress interpretation

Stats should not only show numbers. The app should help the user understand what the numbers mean.

Possible future messages:

- your total increased this cycle
- your squat is trending up
- your bench is stable but not moving yet
- your deadlift e1RM dropped after a hard week
- your bodyweight changed while strength stayed stable
- your meet total estimate improved

Goal: make progress understandable, not just visible.

### 4. Better cycle review

At the end of a cycle, Kelani should summarize what happened.

Possible review items:

- starting total vs current total
- best lift improvements
- completed workouts
- failed/adjusted sets
- bodyweight trend
- next-cycle suggestion

Goal: the user knows whether the cycle worked.

### 5. Better stagnation handling

A failed set should not feel like the program is broken.

Kelani should communicate calmly:

- failing a set is normal
- the plan has been adjusted
- the user can keep training
- long-term progress matters more than one missed set

Goal: keep the lifter training productively after setbacks.

### 6. Better feedback loop

Kelani needs more real-world feedback without analytics or tracking.

Possible improvements:

- clearer feedback option in Settings
- feedback prompt after completing a cycle
- simple email feedback option
- GitHub issue option for technical users
- copy that explains feedback helps shape the app

Goal: receive useful feedback while keeping privacy principles intact.

## v1.2 and later

Bigger ideas should come after the guidance foundation is stronger.

Possible later directions:

- program paths
  - beginner strength
  - powerlifting progression
  - meet preparation
  - manual/custom
- custom program builder
- improved program overview
- richer progress insights
- better body composition interpretation
- optional coaching/review workflow
- improved website documentation
- clearer comparison of training goals

The custom program builder is important, but it should not come too early if it makes the app harder to use.

## What Kelani should avoid

Kelani should avoid:

- becoming cluttered
- adding features without a clear use case
- hiding core usefulness behind payment pressure
- requiring accounts
- adding tracking by default
- copying other apps instead of learning from them
- making workout execution slower
- becoming too generic

Kelani should not try to become every fitness app.

Kelani should stay focused on powerlifting.

## Success definition

Kelani becomes stronger if users feel:

- they know what to do today
- they understand why they are doing it
- they can continue after beginner progress slows down
- they trust the app during training
- they can prepare for a meet
- they can see and understand progress
- they can use the app privately
- the app feels built by a lifter, for lifters

## Strategic summary

Kelani’s next step is not more complexity.

Kelani’s next step is better guidance.

The product direction after v1.0:

> Make Kelani SBD Tracker a calm, practical, privacy-respecting powerlifting guide that stays simple in the gym and becomes smarter around training.
